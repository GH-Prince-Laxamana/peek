What Should Stay Private?

Not everything online should be shared. Some information is personal, and if exposed, it can put your security, identity, and even finances at risk. Learning what to keep private is the first step to protecting yourself online.

Key Information to Protect

Type of Information Why It Matters Examples
Identity Details Prevents identity theft Full name, birthday, address
Financial Information Protects your money Bank account, credit card numbers
Login Credentials Keeps accounts safe Passwords, security questions
Contact Information Avoids spam/scams Phone number, personal email
Health Information Protects your privacy and wellbeing Medical records, health conditions
Location Data Prevents tracking or stalking Home address, GPS data, travel routes
